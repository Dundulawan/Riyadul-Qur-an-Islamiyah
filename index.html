Our History

Riyadhul Qur'an Islamiyya was established in 2014 with the aim of teaching the Noble Qur'an and Islamic studies based on sound Islamic principles. The school started with 5 teachers and 35 students, holding classes on Saturdays and Sundays from 9:00 a.m. to 12:00 noon.

By the grace of Allah, the school has experienced remarkable growth over the years and now operates three academic sections: Tahfeez, Islamiyyah, and Evening classes.

Special Schedules

Ramadan Schedule: Classes adjusted to accommodate fasting students. Shorter sessions with emphasis on Qur'an recitation, memorization, and Islamic lessons. Extra spiritual programs such as Tafsir, Du'a, and prayers may be included.

Holiday Schedule: School observes national and Islamic holidays. Catch-up or revision sessions may be arranged before or after long breaks. Special activities or seminars may be held during holidays to enhance learning.

Admission Process

Join our Islamic learning community with a simple enrollment process

Admission Requirements & Process

  1. Application Submission

    Parents or guardians submit a completed application form along with required documents (birth certificate, previous school records, and passport-sized photo).

  2. Screening / Assessment

    Students are assessed for basic Qur'an reading ability, age suitability, and readiness for memorization or Islamic studies.

  3. Interview / Parent Meeting

    A brief meeting with parents or guardians is held to discuss school rules, curriculum, and expectations.

  4. Enrollment / Registration

    Successful candidates are enrolled and given a timetable, school materials, and instructions for joining classes.

  5. Orientation

    New students and parents attend a short orientation session to familiarize them with school policies, schedules, and teachers.

Admission Requirements

  • Completed application form submitted by parent/guardian
  • Birth certificate or proof of age
  • Passport-sized photograph of the student
  • Previous school records (if applicable)
  • Basic Qur'an reading ability (for Tahfeez applicants)
  • Commitment to regular attendance, discipline, and good character
  • Parental/guardian consent and support
